# Getting the video out (screen-recording)

The clips are animations that play in a web browser. To get a video you can post, you record the screen while it plays. Keep this simple for the user - give them the one method for their computer, not all of them.

## Mac (the easy way)

1. The clip is open and looping in the browser.
2. Press **Shift + Command + 5**.
3. Click **Record Selected Portion**.
4. Drag a box tightly around the clip (the moving part only).
5. Click **Record**. Let it loop once or twice. Click the **stop** button in the menu bar.
6. The video saves to the **Desktop**. Done - that's postable.

Tip: to trim the ends, open it in the **Photos** or **QuickTime** app and drag the edges.

## Windows

1. The clip is open and looping in the browser.
2. Press **Windows + G** to open Xbox Game Bar, then the **record** button (or **Windows + Alt + R**).
3. Let it loop, then stop.
4. Find it in **Videos > Captures**.

(Game Bar records the whole window; crop to the clip later in any editor, or use a region recorder like ShareX.)

## Cleaner / higher quality (optional, if they ask)

- **Exact 9:16 crop:** in Chrome, open DevTools (Cmd/Ctrl+Shift+I), click the phone icon (device toolbar), add a custom device sized **1080 x 1920**, then record just that frame.
- **Dedicated recorders:** CleanShot X, ScreenFlow, OBS, or ShareX let you set a fixed 1080x1920 capture region for crisp results.

## Good habits

- Let it loop once before you start recording so you begin on a clean cycle.
- Record 1-2 full loops, then trim to one clean loop.
- Vertical (taller than wide) is what you want for Reels / TikTok / Stories.
